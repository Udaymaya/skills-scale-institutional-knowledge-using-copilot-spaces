# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
The Product Owner is accountable for defining the product vision, prioritizing the backlog, and representing stakeholder interests throughout the project lifecycle. They bridge strategic goals and delivery execution.

### Responsibilities
- Own the product vision and communicate it clearly to the team
- Prioritize and maintain the product backlog based on business value
- Define and validate acceptance criteria for user stories
- Act as the primary point of contact for stakeholder requirements
- Make trade-off decisions balancing business goals and technical feasibility

### Goals
- Ensure the team delivers maximum business and customer value
- Maintain a well-groomed, clearly prioritized backlog
- Align product direction with stakeholder expectations and market needs

### Typical Communication
- Sprint reviews and backlog refinement sessions
- Stakeholder briefings and requirement workshops
- Collaboration with Project Managers on timeline and scope trade-offs

### Cross-Role Collaboration
- Works with **Project Managers** to balance scope, schedule, and resources
- Aligns with **Business Analysts** on requirements discovery and story refinement
- Partners with **Developers** to clarify acceptance criteria and feature intent

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead is responsible for defining quality benchmarks, planning testing strategies, and serving as the final reviewer before release. They coordinate across development and release teams to ensure quality standards are consistently met.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality metrics, test plans, and acceptance criteria for releases
- Manage the QA cycle including unit, integration, and acceptance testing
- Oversee sign-off on release readiness
- Identify and track defects, ensuring timely resolution
- Partner with engineering and release teams to uphold quality standards

### Goals
- Achieve high defect detection rates before production
- Ensure all releases meet acceptance criteria and performance benchmarks
- Continuously improve testing practices and coverage

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews and QA status updates in sprint ceremonies
- Defect triage meetings with developers
- Release readiness sign-off with Project Managers and Product Owners

### Cross-Role Collaboration
- Partners with **Developers** to define test cases and resolve defects
- Coordinates with **Project Managers** on release schedules and risk assessment
- Aligns with **Product Owners** to validate acceptance criteria

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
The Business Analyst bridges gaps between technical and non-technical stakeholders, gathers and documents requirements, and translates business needs into actionable tasks for the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, analyze, and document business requirements
- Translate business needs into user stories and acceptance criteria
- Facilitate workshops and discovery sessions with stakeholders
- Identify process gaps and propose improvements
- Maintain requirement traceability throughout the project lifecycle

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are complete, clear, and actionable
- Reduce ambiguity and rework caused by unclear specifications
- Foster shared understanding between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and discovery sessions
- User story grooming with Product Owners and Developers
- Status reporting to Project Managers and stakeholders

### Cross-Role Collaboration
- Works closely with **Product Owners** to align requirements with product vision
- Collaborates with **Project Managers** to surface dependencies and risks
- Engages **Developers** during story refinement to clarify technical constraints

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
The Agile Coach facilitates agile best practices across teams, advises on process improvements, and guides retrospectives and continuous improvement initiatives. They help OctoAcme teams adopt and sustain effective agile ways of working.

### Responsibilities
- Coach teams on scrum, kanban, and other agile frameworks
- Lead and facilitate retrospectives, planning, and review ceremonies
- Identify process bottlenecks and recommend improvements
- Foster a culture of psychological safety, feedback, and learning
- Guide teams through organizational change and scaling challenges

### Goals
- Increase team velocity and delivery predictability
- Embed a culture of continuous improvement across all roles
- Remove impediments to agile adoption and effective collaboration

### Typical Communication
- Retrospective and improvement action-item sessions
- One-on-one coaching with team leads and Project Managers
- Workshops and training sessions on agile practices

### Cross-Role Collaboration
- Advises **Project Managers** on process optimization and ceremony facilitation
- Supports **Product Owners** in backlog management and stakeholder engagement
- Guides **Developers** and **QA Leads** in adopting agile engineering practices
